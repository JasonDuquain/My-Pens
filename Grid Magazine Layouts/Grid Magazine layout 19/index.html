360–degree infrared vision

Invention: Thermal Radar

Development cost to date

Market Maturity

How it works

1

Warm objects—people, car engines, tires etc.—emit infrared light.

2

A spinning camera takes up to 18 thermal images per second, eliminating the need for multiple, expensive cameras.

3

Software stitches the images together and beat signatures are triangulated with GPS to show their location as a blip on a radar–like applet.
